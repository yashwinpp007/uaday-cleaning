Keeping your home clean doesn't have to mean filling it with harsh chemicals. More and more Melbourne families are making the switch to eco-friendly cleaning — and discovering that it works just as well (sometimes better!) than conventional products.

At UaDay Cleaning, we've been using certified eco-friendly products exclusively for over 10 years. Here are five of our favourite tips for a cleaner, greener home.

## 1. White Vinegar Is Your Best Friend

White vinegar is one of the most versatile eco-friendly cleaning agents available. It's antibacterial, cuts through grease, and removes mineral deposits — all for a fraction of the cost of commercial cleaners.

**Best uses:**
- Glass and mirrors (streak-free shine)
- Cutting through grease on stovetops
- Descaling kettle and coffee makers
- Removing soap scum in bathrooms

**Mix it with water** (1:1 ratio) in a spray bottle for a general-purpose cleaner. Add a few drops of essential oil (eucalyptus works brilliantly) to cut the vinegar smell.

> ⚠️ **Do not use vinegar on marble, granite, or natural stone** — the acid can etch the surface.

## 2. Baking Soda for Scrubbing and Odour Absorption

Baking soda is mildly abrasive and excellent at absorbing odours. It's completely non-toxic and safe to use anywhere in the home.

**Best uses:**
- Scrubbing the oven (paste with water)
- Removing stains from grout
- Freshening carpets (sprinkle and vacuum)
- Deodorising the fridge
- Unclogging drains (combine with vinegar for a fizzing reaction)

## 3. Look for Australian-Certified Eco Products

When buying commercial cleaning products, look for:

- **Good Environmental Choice Australia (GECA)** certified products
- **Environmental Choice Australia** certification
- Products free from phosphates, chlorine bleach, and synthetic fragrances

Australian-made eco-friendly brands like Earth Choice, Aware, and Seventh Generation offer excellent performance without the environmental impact.

## 4. Microfibre Cloths Over Paper Towels

Switching to high-quality microfibre cloths is one of the single most impactful eco changes you can make in your cleaning routine. Microfibre:

- Traps dust, bacteria, and grime without chemicals
- Can clean most surfaces with just water
- Lasts hundreds of washes before needing replacement
- Dramatically reduces paper towel waste

Dedicate different colours to different rooms (e.g., red for bathrooms, blue for kitchen) to avoid cross-contamination.

## 5. Use Concentrated Products to Reduce Plastic Waste

Many eco-friendly brands now sell concentrated formulas that you dilute yourself. A small bottle of concentrate can replace 5–10 regular-sized bottles — dramatically reducing plastic packaging.

Better yet, look for products that come in:
- **Refillable packaging** systems
- **Dissolvable tablet** formats (just add water)
- **Glass or cardboard** containers

## The Bottom Line

An eco-friendly home isn't just better for the environment — it's better for your family. Children and pets are especially vulnerable to chemical residues left on surfaces by conventional cleaners.
