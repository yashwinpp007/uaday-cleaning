Moving out of a rental property in Melbourne is stressful enough without the added anxiety of losing your bond. Your end of lease inspection is your final chance to leave the property in the same condition it was when you moved in — and it can make the difference between getting your full bond back or losing hundreds (sometimes thousands) of dollars.

Here's everything you need to know to pass your inspection with flying colours.

## What is an End of Lease Inspection?

An end of lease inspection (also called a vacate inspection) is a formal assessment carried out by your real estate agent or property manager after you've moved out. They compare the property's current condition against the original condition report to identify any damage or cleaning deficiencies.

If the property doesn't meet the required standard, your landlord can make a claim against your bond.

## Start with the Condition Report

Your very first step should be digging out the original condition report from when you moved in. This document is your bible for the inspection. Go room by room and check every item listed.

Pay close attention to anything that was already noted as "fair" or "poor" condition — you only need to return the property to the state it was in, not better.

## The End of Lease Cleaning Checklist

### Kitchen
- Clean the oven inside and out (this is the most commonly failed item)
- Degrease the rangehood and filters
- Wipe all cupboards inside and out
- Clean the stovetop, benches, and splashback
- Descale the sink and taps

### Bathrooms
- Scrub the toilet, including under the rim
- Remove soap scum and limescale from tiles and grout
- Polish mirrors streak-free
- Clean exhaust fans
- Wipe down vanity and storage

### Living Areas and Bedrooms
- Vacuum all carpets (steam clean if required by your lease)
- Mop hard floors
- Dust all surfaces, including skirting boards and architraves
- Wipe light switches and door handles
- Clean window sills and tracks
- Remove any marks from walls

### Outside and Garage
- Sweep and hose down outdoor areas
- Clean the garage floor

## What to Do on Inspection Day

1. **Be present if possible** — you can address any concerns on the spot
2. **Have all receipts ready** — including your professional cleaning invoice
3. **Do a final walkthrough** before the agent arrives
4. **Take photos** of every room as evidence of the condition

## Common Reasons Bonds Are Withheld

According to Consumer Affairs Victoria, the most common reasons landlords claim against bonds are:

1. **Cleaning** (by far the most common — accounts for ~60% of bond disputes)
2. **Damage to property**
3. **Unpaid rent**
4. **General maintenance**
